**C1:** Subjunctive, inversion, cleft sentences, mixed conditionals, advanced passive  
**C2:** Nuanced language, idiomatic expressions, register shifts, discourse markers

## 📊 CONTENT SCALING BY LEVEL (50h vs 150h)

When creating 50h versions, reduce content depth but maintain breadth:

**150h Course:**
- 12 lessons
- 5 modules
- 8-12 vocabulary words per lesson (100+ total)
- 6-8 grammar structures
- Deep practice (10+ activities per lesson)

**50h Course:**
- 6-8 lessons
- 3 modules
- 6-8 vocabulary words per lesson (50-60 total)
- 4-5 grammar structures
- Focused practice (5-6 activities per lesson)

**What to Keep:**
- Same overall CEFR level target
- Same professional HR contexts
- Same evaluation rigor

**What to Reduce:**
- Number of examples per grammar point
- Number of practice exercises
- Depth of reading/listening texts (shorter)
- Homework load

## 🚀 PRODUCTION WORKFLOW

### **Recommended Order:**

1. **Complete all 150h Async proposals first** (A1-C2)
   - Use existing A1 as template
   - Focus on getting modules right (most work)
   - Can reuse sections 1-6, 9-22 with minor edits

2. **Then create 50h Sync proposals** (A1-C2)
   - Copy from 150h async of same level
   - Apply sync modifications (modality, methodology, duration)
   - Condense modules from 5→3

### **Time Estimate:**
- **Per 150h Async proposal:** 2-3 hours (after A1 is complete)
- **Per 50h Sync proposal:** 1-2 hours (copying from 150h + modifications)
- **Total:** 20-30 hours for all 12 proposals

### **Efficiency Tips:**
1. Create a "variables" file with all the changing elements per level
2. Use batch find-replace in a good text editor (VS Code, Sublime)
3. Keep a checklist of sections that need level-specific updates
4. Have grammar/vocab lists prepared beforehand
5. Copy-paste evaluation rubrics (they're mostly the same)

## 💡 QUICK REFERENCE: CEFR CAN-DO STATEMENTS FOR OBJECTIVES

**A1 (Breakthrough):**
Can understand and use familiar everyday expressions and very basic phrases. Can introduce themselves and ask/answer questions about personal details. Can interact in a simple way.

**A2 (Waystage):**
Can understand sentences and frequently used expressions related to areas of immediate relevance. Can communicate in simple routine tasks. Can describe matters of immediate need.

**B1 (Threshold):**
Can understand the main points of clear standard input on familiar matters. Can deal with most situations likely to arise while working. Can produce simple connected text on familiar topics. Can describe experiences, events, dreams, hopes.

**B2 (Vantage):**
Can understand the main ideas of complex text on concrete and abstract topics, including technical discussions. Can interact with fluency and spontaneity. Can produce clear, detailed text on a wide range of subjects.

**C1 (Effective Operational Proficiency):**
Can understand a wide range of demanding, longer texts and recognize implicit meaning. Can express themselves fluently without much obvious searching for expressions. Can use language flexibly and effectively for social, academic and professional purposes.

**C2 (Mastery):**
Can understand with ease virtually everything heard or read. Can summarize information from different spoken and written sources, reconstructing arguments coherently. Can express themselves spontaneously, very fluently and precisely, differentiating finer shades of meaning even in complex situations.
